1. Why PILa?

Raw robot trajectories record joint angles, forces, and poses as low-level time series. They tell us what changed, but not why the interaction failed:

  • Which phase broke down?
  • What physical relation degraded?
  • What invariant was violated?
  • What patch should be suggested and validated next?

PILa is a Physical Interaction Language for embodied intelligence. It describes what happens in physical interaction, not merely what the robot did. AS-IR is the intermediate representation layer underneath PILa — it structures intent, phases, physical relations, failure hypotheses, patch suggestions, transferability, runtime risks and engineering adapters.

2. Raw Trajectory vs PILa / AS-IR Trace

Raw trajectory is signal-level. PILa uses AS-IR to provide structure-level.

3. Raw Trajectory View

This chart compares the failed run and the patched run of the cup-grasp task. In the failed run, the grip force remains too low, the slip score rises sharply, the cup tilt increases, the contact force drops, and the cup only lifts briefly before falling. In the patched run, the grip force is increased, the contact force remains stable, the slip score stays close to zero, and the cup height continues to rise. This indicates that the patch improves support stability during grasping and lifting. However, raw trajectory data only shows which signals changed. It does not directly explain why the physical interaction failed. AS-IR / PILa adds this missing structural layer: during the grasp → lift phase, the support relation between the gripper and the cup degrades from stable support to unstable support and eventually breaks. The failure hypothesis is not a single abnormal signal, but the failure to maintain support stability under low-friction conditions.

Each robot's morphology adapter translates this meaning into its own executable actions. A two-finger gripper increases force; a three-finger gripper adds a contact point; a suction gripper increases vacuum pressure.

This is a minimal representation test for cross-embodiment meaning transfer. It does not prove real robot-to-robot transfer works.

13. Key Hypothesis

This MVP does not prove PILa solves embodied intelligence.
It only tests a minimal hypothesis:

A physical interaction language, implemented through AS-IR, can make physical interaction states, failure hypotheses, patch suggestions, validation results, and learning updates more explicit than raw trajectory alone.

What v0.5.2 adds:

  • Per-phase risk levels (GREEN / YELLOW / ORANGE)
  • Patch validation pipeline (pending → passed)
  • Representation gain quantification
  • Run metadata for reproducibility tracking
  • Cross-embodiment meaning transfer (same failure meaning, different robot patches)
  • Interactive animations: physical interaction timeline, meaning extraction, cross-embodiment flow
  • Bilingual report: English / 中文 toggle
